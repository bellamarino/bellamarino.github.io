Lucie Aubrac’s dress on the day she met with Gestapo Chief Klaus Barbie, was a calculated choice to disguise herself as an upper class, sympathetic woman who had been taken advantage of. In reality, she was a resistant, and her story was an effort to break her husband out of prison. 

Aubrac described herself wearing, “a very pretty checkered rayon suit, big white porcelain daisy earrings, and a tiny pillbox hat with a little veil.”
